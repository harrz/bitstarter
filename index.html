Easy signup.

Sign up yourself and your roomates with their email addresses and/or phonenumbers. They'll receive a notification message that they joined the game with an option to opt-out.

Define Chores.

Create new chores, add frequency and responsibility. These chores will constitute the basis of your game. Here are some examples:
Cleaning the toilet | Frequency: Every two weeks | Responsibility: Alex & Tom
Doing the dishes | Frequency: Every other day | Responsibilty: Everyone

Get notified.

Sign up yourself and your roomates with their email addresses and/or phonenumbers. They'll\ receive a notification message that they joined the game with an option to opt-out.

Win the Game.

Check off chores you've completed and receive points based on the difficulty of the chore (Easy, Medium, Hard, Super Yuck) and how fast you got it done.

Visualize your superiority (and their laziness).

A chart says more than a thousand words. Lazy people oftentimes don't even realize the extent of their laziness. Demonstrating it to them proves their laziness.

Turn up the heat.

Add your roommates mom or girlfriend (or whomever your roommate fears the most) as a spectator. Spectators will receive periodic updates on the state of the game.

Post the results of your game to Facebook and publicly shame them into productivity.

FAQ

How to avoid disagreements and confrontations?
When starting your game, you'll be able to define an impartial referee, who will resolve your disputes. Ideally, this be mutual friend of yours.
Isn't this just spam?
In order not to be classified as spam, your roommates will always have the ability to opt-out of notifications.
